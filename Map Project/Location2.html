Seattle is a West Coast seaport city and the seat of King County, Washington. With an estimated 684,451 residents as of 2015, Seattle is the largest city in both the state of Washington and the Pacific Northwest region of North America. The Seattle metropolitan area is the 15th largest metropolitan area in the United States with over 3.7 million inhabitants. A major gateway for trade with Asia, Seattle is the third largest port in North America in terms of container handling as of 2015. Arthur A. Denny and his group of travelers, arrived from Illinois via Portland, Oregon, on the schooner Exact at Alki Point on November 13, 1851. The settlement was moved to the eastern shore of Elliott Bay and named "Seattle" in 1852, after Chief Si'ahl of the local Duwamish and Suquamish tribes.
